 The July Movement originated as a student-led campaign against the 30% civil service quota, advocating for a merit-based system. Following the tragic stand of Abu Sayed, the protest transformed into a nationwide uprising against systemic inequality and state-led crackdowns. Despite a total internet blackout and strict curfews, the movement unified millions under a "One-Point Demand." This collective defiance culminated on August 5, 2024, when the government resigned. Now hailed as a "Second Independence," it marks a pivotal shift toward democratic reform and youth-led governance in Bangladesh.              

  the movement gained unstoppable momentum following the tragic sacrifice of students like Abu Sayed, whose bravery became a symbol of defiance against systemic oppression.
